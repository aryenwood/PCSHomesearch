Things Nobody Tells You

The insider knowledge that saves military families thousands.

This is the kind of stuff you only learn after living at Fort Drum. We're giving it to you before you get here.

Natural gas vs. propane = $1,500/year difference
Watertown city has natural gas lines. Evans Mills, Black River, and most rural areas do not — they're on propane or oil, which costs $1,000–2,000 more per year. We know which streets have gas.
Two homes on the same street can be in different school districts
Fort Drum spans 3 districts: General Brown (most popular), Indian River, and Carthage. The boundary lines are confusing. Confirm the district before you make an offer — not after.
Register for CYSS childcare the day you get orders
The waitlist is 3–6 months. Register at militarychildcare.com before you arrive. If you wait until you get here, you'll be scrambling for months.
NY military spouses get automatic license reciprocity
New York honors out-of-state licenses for military spouses — nursing, teaching, real estate, and 10+ professions. You get a 6-month temporary permit while your transfer processes.
Veterans get 3 property tax exemptions in NY — most people miss 2 of them
NY has Alternative Veterans (458-a), Cold War Veterans (458-b), and Eligible Funds (458) exemptions. Combined with STAR, you could save $1,000+ per year on property taxes. Most families only file for one.
Snow tires aren't optional — and AWD matters here
140+ inches of snow per year. All-season tires won't cut it. Budget for a set of snow tires ($400–800) and strongly consider 4WD/AWD. Studded tires are legal Oct 16 – Apr 30 in NY.
