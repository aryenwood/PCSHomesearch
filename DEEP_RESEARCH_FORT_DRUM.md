## 1. SCHOOL ENROLLMENT -- All 3 Districts

### New York Interstate Compact (MIC3) -- Applies to ALL Districts

New York adopted the Interstate Compact on Educational Opportunity for Military Children in 2014 (Education Law Section 3304). This is critical for every PCSing family to know:

- **Advance enrollment is REQUIRED to be offered.** NY school districts must permit preliminary enrollment by remote registration, without charge, for students covered under the Compact. You do NOT need to be physically present to start the enrollment process.
- **Residency is assumed.** A student whose parent/guardian is being relocated under military orders and is transferred or pending transfer to a military installation in NY is deemed a resident pupil for enrollment purposes.
- **IEP/Special Education transfers:** A child with an IEP or Section 504 Plan must receive comparable services and reasonable accommodations until the receiving school can re-evaluate the child. Schools must comply with IDEA and Part 200 of the Commissioner's regulations.
- **Grade and course placement:** Students must initially be placed at the same grade level as the sending state. Honors, AP, IB, vocational, and career pathway courses from the sending school must be honored if offered and space is available.

### New York State Immunization Requirements (All Districts)

Per New York State law, children must be immunized against the following to attend school: Diphtheria, Tetanus, Pertussis (DTaP), Polio (IPV), Measles, Mumps, Rubella (MMR), Hepatitis B, and Varicella (Chickenpox). Specific dose requirements depend on grade level. New York does NOT allow religious exemptions for school immunizations (as of June 2019). Only valid medical exemptions are accepted.

## 2. NY STATE MILITARY FAMILY BENEFITS

### SCRA (Servicemembers Civil Relief Act) -- Lease Termination in NY

**Federal SCRA protections (50 USC Section 3955):**
- Applies to active duty, activated Reserve, and National Guard under federal orders
- Allows early termination of residential leases when receiving PCS orders or deployment orders lasting 90+ days
- **Written notice required:** Deliver by hand, mail, or verified email, and attach a copy of official orders
- **Effective date:** Termination becomes effective 30 days after the next rent due date following delivery of notice
- **No penalties:** Landlords CANNOT charge early termination fees or penalties
- **Security deposit:** Must be returned according to state law. Prepaid rent must be prorated and refunded within 30 days

**New York State Military Law Section 310 (State-Level Protection):**
NY Military Law Section 310 provides an additional state-level safeguard that mirrors the federal SCRA and lays out timing rules tied to the rent-due cycle. This means you have BOTH federal and state protections. New York codifies these protections so courts and landlords apply them consistently rather than relying solely on federal enforcement.

### NY Military Spouse Professional License Reciprocity

**Current Law (Effective June 28, 2023):**
Members of the U.S. military or spouses of U.S. military service members can apply for temporary certification to practice professionally in New York State.

**Professions Covered (under NYSED Office of the Professions):**
- Architects
- Dentists
- **Nurses** (RN, LPN)
- Pharmacists
- Physicians
- Veterinarians
- **Teachers** (expedited review for spouses holding valid license in another state)
- **Real estate brokers and salespersons** (Military Spouse Waiver Application via NY DOS)
- Apartment vendors
- Appearance enhancement professionals
- Athletic agents
- Bail enforcement agents
- Home inspectors

**How It Works:**
- A Temporary Practice Permit lets you begin working under the supervision of a currently registered NYS licensee
- The permit is valid for **6 months** (or until 10 days after notification that qualifications are not met)
- Military spouses transferred to NY receive expedited review if they hold a license in good standing in another state with substantially equivalent requirements

**Pending Legislation (2025):**
Assembly Bill A5960 (introduced February 25, 2025) -- the "Military Spouse Act" -- would permit a military spouse to practice an occupation upon submission of licensure from ANY other state, provided licensing standards are not lower than New York's. If passed, this would significantly expand portability.

### Veterans & Military Property Tax Exemptions

New York has THREE separate property tax exemptions for veterans. You may qualify for one or more:

**1. Alternative Veterans Exemption (RPTL Section 458-a) -- MOST COMMON**
- **Wartime service:** 15% of assessed value
- **Combat zone service:** Additional 10% (total 25%)
- **Disability:** Additional exemption equal to 50% of service-connected disability rating
- **Maximum possible:** 75% exemption (wartime + combat zone + 100% disability)
- **Application:** File Form RP-458-a with your municipal assessor
- **Deadline:** March 1 in most communities (confirm with your assessor)
- **Required documents:** DD-214 (honorable discharge) or letter from NY DVS under the Restoration of Honor Act

**2. Cold War Veterans Exemption (RPTL Section 458-b)**
- **Basic exemption:** 10% or 15% of assessed value (community dependent)
- **Disability add-on:** Assessed value x 50% of disability rating (up to $40,000 cap)
- **Duration:** Basic exemption limited to 10 years unless locality makes it indefinite; disability portion is permanent
- **Application:** File Form RP-458-b with your municipal assessor

**3. Eligible Funds Exemption (RPTL Section 458)**
- For properties purchased with eligible funds (VA compensation, pension, insurance proceeds)
- Exemption based on amount of eligible funds used in purchase

**Who can apply:** Veteran, spouse of veteran, or unremarried surviving spouse of deceased veteran. Property must be primary residence.

### STAR Exemption / Credit for Homebuyers

**Important note for new homebuyers:** The STAR *exemption* is no longer available to new homeowners. If you purchased your home after 2015, you receive your benefit as a **STAR Credit check** or direct deposit from the NY State Tax Department.

- **Income limit:** $110,750 for 2026 (based on 2024 income)

- **Military retirement pay:** FULLY EXEMPT from NY State, NYC, and Yonkers income tax. Includes regular retirement, reserve retirement, and disability retirement from all branches. No age restrictions. No income limits.

## 3. NEIGHBORHOOD HEATING -- Propane vs. Natural Gas

### The Short Answer

**Watertown city proper:** Natural gas is available through National Grid in most of the city. The City of Watertown also operates its own municipal electric utility.

**Evans Mills, Black River, Carthage, and most rural areas around Fort Drum:** These communities are primarily served by **propane and heating oil**, not natural gas. Multiple propane delivery companies serve these areas, which strongly suggests natural gas pipeline infrastructure is limited or absent.

**Liberty Utilities** (formerly St. Lawrence Gas) serves natural gas to areas in St. Lawrence, Lewis, and Franklin Counties -- including parts of Lewis County near Carthage -- but their service territory does not comprehensively cover Jefferson County communities near Fort Drum.

### Natural Gas vs. Propane by Area

| Location | Primary Heating Fuel | Natural Gas Available? |
|---|---|---|
| **Watertown (city)** | Natural gas | Yes -- National Grid serves most of the city |
| **Evans Mills** | Propane / Heating oil | Limited or unavailable -- multiple propane companies serve the area |
| **Black River** | Propane / Heating oil | Limited or unavailable |
| **Carthage** | Propane / Heating oil; some natural gas | Possible in some areas via Liberty Utilities (Lewis County) |
| **Dexter** | Propane / Heating oil | Limited or unavailable |
| **On-post housing** | Utilities included in rent | N/A -- MCH covers all utilities |

### How to Confirm Before You Sign a Lease or Buy

1. **National Grid Service Check:** [nationalgridus.com/CheckServiceAvailability](https://www1.nationalgridus.com/CheckServiceAvailability-StartService-NYL-RES) -- Enter the specific address
2. **Liberty Utilities:** [stlawrencegas.com](https://www.stlawrencegas.com/) -- Call to confirm coverage
3. **Ask the landlord/seller directly:** "What type of heating does this home use? When was the furnace last serviced? How large is the propane tank?"

### Cost Comparison (Real Numbers)

**Propane (2024-2025 heating season, NY statewide average):**
- Average residential price: approximately **$3.43/gallon** (December 2025, NY statewide)
- Northeast regional average: $2.80/gallon (April 2025)
- Typical annual usage for a North Country home: **1,100+ gallons**
- **Estimated annual propane cost: $3,000 - $4,000** (varies by home size, insulation, thermostat habits)

**Natural gas (National Grid, upstate NY):**
- Average cost: approximately $1.84/therm (Northeast, April 2025)
- **Estimated annual natural gas cost: $1,200 - $2,000** for a comparable home

**Bottom line:** Natural gas homes can save **$1,000 - $2,000/year** compared to propane. If you have the choice, natural gas is significantly cheaper.

### Propane Pre-Buy Programs

- **Enrollment window:** Typically August 1 through October 1
- Pre-buy locks in a fixed price per gallon for the entire heating season
- Local propane providers: Christman Fuel Service, Glider Oil, Heller's Gas, Suburban Propane, AmeriGas, Ferrellgas
- **NYSERDA tracks regional pricing:** [nyserda.ny.gov/Energy-Prices/Propane](https://www.nyserda.ny.gov/Energy-Prices/Propane/Average-Propane-Prices)

## 5. WINTER SURVIVAL -- SPECIFICS

### Average Snowfall

- **Watertown:** ~102-112 inches per year
- **Fort Drum:** ~101-112 inches per year
- Fort Drum is in the **lake-effect snow belt** off Lake Ontario, making it one of the snowiest locations in the United States
- Lake-effect snow can dump **12-18 inches in a matter of hours** in very localized bands
- Snow season runs roughly November through April

### Snow Tires and Chains

**Do you need snow tires? YES.**
Per PCS guides written by military families: "Snow tires are not optional -- all-season tires are inadequate and unsafe in a North Country winter. Invest in a set of dedicated snow tires."

**Tire chains:**
- **Legal in New York** during hazardous road conditions, as long as they do not damage the highway surface
- Required when a street/highway is designated as a "snow emergency route" (unless you have snow tires)
- No state law specifying number of chains or placement
- **Studded tires** are legal October 16 through April 30

**In practice:** Most families use dedicated winter tires (swapped onto a second set of rims) rather than chains. Chains are a backup measure, not daily-use equipment.

### What Vehicle Do Families Actually Drive?

**AWD or 4WD is strongly recommended.** From military family guides: "Driving a RWD sedan in a foot of lake-effect snow at 0630 is a miserable and dangerous experience."

Popular vehicles among Fort Drum families:
- **Subaru Forester / Outback / Ascent** -- AWD standard, excellent in snow
- **Ford Escape AWD**
- **Nissan Rogue SV AWD**
- **Toyota RAV4 AWD**
- AWD crossovers and SUVs in general

You do NOT need a full-size truck, but if your current vehicle is rear-wheel drive, seriously reconsider it for North Country winters.

### Heating Preparation

**When to get your furnace serviced:**
- **Before heating season** -- schedule in August or September
- Propane heating systems should be serviced annually (condensation and particulate buildup)
- Annual tune-ups increase equipment lifespan and efficiency

**Propane pre-buy:**
- **Enroll August 1 - October 1** for the upcoming heating season
- Locks in a fixed price per gallon all winter
- Typical household uses 1,100+ gallons annually
- If on automatic delivery, the provider calculates when to refill
- If on will-call delivery, **request refill when tank gauge hits 1/3 full**

**Winter heating costs:** Expect **$200-300/month** for an off-post home heated with propane.

### Snow Removal

**On-post (Mountain Community Homes):**
- **DPW/Public Works** plows roads -- do NOT park in the street to allow plow access
- **Residents** are responsible for clearing their own driveways and walkways
- MCH provides overall community snow removal for common areas

**Off-post (Watertown city):**
- City of Watertown Public Works handles main streets, school areas, and known problem areas
- **Sidewalk and driveway clearing is the homeowner/tenant's responsibility**
- Local snow removal companies: Lakeside Outdoor Services, Marzano Excavating, A Cut Above Landscape, Ben Shoen's Home Improvement
- **Budget $300-600/season** for a driveway snow removal contract (varies by driveway size)

**Off-post (rural areas outside city -- Evans Mills, etc.):**
- County/town plows public roads (may take longer than city streets)
- You are responsible for your own driveway -- consider a plow service or buying a snowblower

### School Snow Day Policy

- Fort Drum does NOT directly control school closures -- local school districts make their own decisions
- Fort Drum's winter weather call policy balances mission and safety; the installation informs local school districts about on-post conditions
- Indian River CSD and other districts monitor weather conditions and make delay/closure decisions independently
- **Typical frequency:** Multiple snow days and delays per year (exact number varies by winter severity)
- School closings are announced via local media ([InformNNY.com](https://www.informnny.com/community/nny-school-zone/closings-delays-and-early-dismissals-so-far-for-nny/)) and district websites/apps

### Essential Winter Gear for the Family

- Proper winter coat rated for sub-zero temperatures
- Waterproof insulated boots
- Quality gloves, hats, and layers
- **Find a winter hobby** -- the winters are long, and families who stay active fare much better mentally

**The payoff:** "The summers are absolutely glorious and feel earned after a long winter."

### Dead Zones and Known Issues

- **On-post buildings:** User reports indicate spotty coverage INSIDE buildings on Fort Drum. One review: "Only way to get a call is to stand outside." Another: "Excellent reception all through Fort Drum Cantonment Area" -- so it varies by location and building construction.
- **Rural areas of Jefferson County:** Most of the county (99.7% of land area) has some wireless coverage, but rural pockets between towns can have weak signal
- **Best practice:** Get a Wi-Fi calling-capable phone. This solves most indoor coverage issues if your home internet is reliable.

### Electric Service

- **City of Watertown:** Municipal electric utility (city-owned) -- often lower rates than investor-owned utilities
- **National Grid:** Serves areas outside Watertown city proper, including much of Jefferson County
- Fort Drum on-post: electric included in MCH rent
