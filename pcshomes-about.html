The Story Behind PCSHomes

I grew up here.
I know what these families need.

My name is Joseph Murtha, and I've spent my entire life in the communities surrounding Fort Drum. I was born and raised here, in the same towns that thousands of military families PCS into every year — often with little more than a report date and a Google Maps search to guide them.

"I watched families arrive in October with no idea what a North Country winter actually meant. I watched soldiers overpay for homes because they didn't know the market, or miss out on their VA benefits because nobody explained them clearly. I knew there had to be a better way."

— Joseph Murtha, Founder

As a real estate agent with North Star Real Estate and a property investor in the Watertown area, I've helped hundreds of military families find homes, navigate VA loans, and settle into this community. But I always felt like the tools available to them — the national platforms, the generic relocation sites — just didn't get it.

They didn't know that Evans Mills is a 4-minute drive from the gate. They didn't know that General Brown Central School District is where most families with kids want to be. They didn't know that the rental market here is strong enough that buying and renting out later is one of the smartest financial moves a soldier can make at Drum.

So I built PCSHomes. Starting with Fort Drum — the place I know best — and eventually expanding to cover every major installation in the country. A platform built not just for finding a house, but for navigating the entire military home journey: the PCS move, the VA loan, the base guide, the community of people who've been there before you.

This is my hometown. These are my neighbors. And I'm going to make sure every family that PCSes here gets treated like it.
