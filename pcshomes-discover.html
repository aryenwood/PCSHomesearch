North Country Living

"Is Fort Drum really that bad?"

We get it — nobody Googles Fort Drum and gets excited. But the families who actually live here? Most of them don't want to leave. Here's what they know that you don't.

Family & Kids
Honest answer: is this a good place to raise kids?
Yes. The military community is tight, the outdoor access is unreal, and your kids will grow up tougher than most. Here's what families actually do here.

CYSS Youth Programs

On-post childcare, youth sports, summer camps, before/after school. Register on MilitaryChildCare.com the day you get orders — the waitlist is 3-6 months and it doesn't start until you sign up.

Parent Central: (315) 772-8675 →

Flower Memorial Library

Watertown's public library is where you'll end up on those -10°F days. Children's section has a puppet theater, costume area, and audiobook center. Story hours weekly. Free.

flowermemoriallibrary.org →

Fort Drum MWR

Indoor pool + swim lessons at Magrath, 24-lane bowling at Pine Plains, Gear to Go equipment rental (boats, skis, bikes, campers). The MWR calendar runs something every week — don't ignore it.

See MWR calendar →

Thompson Park + Zoo New York

435 acres, a zoo with wolves/bears/eagles, public pool, splash pad, playgrounds, hiking trails, and panoramic views. Your kids will beg to go every weekend. The only zoo in the world dedicated to NY-native animals.

zoonewyork.org →

Spouse Community

Fort Drum's spouse community is genuinely one of the strongest in the Army. Your FRG, the North Country Spouses Club, and the Fort Drum Spouses Facebook group will be your lifeline — especially during deployments.

Join the Facebook group before you PCS

Winter Isn't Boring

Snowmobiling on Tug Hill, skiing at Dry Hill, ice fishing, snowshoeing, fat tire biking (rent from MWR). Locally: bowling, ice skating at Watertown Arena, Sci-Tech Center for kids. Destiny USA in Syracuse (90 min) when you need a mall day.

Get Outside
This is why people secretly love getting stationed here
Nobody asks for Fort Drum. But the families who lean into the outdoors? They end up wanting to come back.
Thousand Islands

1,864 islands spanning the US-Canada border. World-class fishing, kayaking, and the iconic Boldt Castle.

1 hr south
Thompson Park

Watertown's crown jewel — 435 acres of trails, a zoo, public pool, playgrounds, and the best views of the city. Military families' go-to park.

In Watertown
Tug Hill Plateau

The snowiest region east of the Rockies. Snowmobiling, cross-country skiing, and 300+ inches of snow per year.

30 min south
Lake Ontario

Charter fishing for salmon and trout. Henderson Harbor and Sackets Harbor are local favorites.

30 min west
Day Trips
Where families actually go on weekends

Sackets Harbor

Historic village on Lake Ontario. Battlefield tours, breweries, galleries, and the annual Can-Am Festival.

Fort Drum MWR

Family pools, youth sports, bowling, arts & crafts, and seasonal kid events — all on post. Don't sleep on the ski lodge at Whiteface for a family day trip.

Watertown Events

First Fridays downtown, Jefferson County Fair, Salmon Run Mall, plus a growing food scene.

Alex Bay & Clayton

Summer vacation towns on the St. Lawrence. Boat tours, ice cream shops, and the Antique Boat Museum.

Local Essentials Map

Everything you need — on one map.

Groceries, doctors, restaurants, storage. Click any pin for the name and address.

Commissary Grocery Medical Restaurants Storage Discount
Grocery
17 stores across 5 towns. Walmart + ALDI near the gate. Hannaford + Price Chopper in Watertown. Commissary on post.
Medical
Guthrie Clinic on post. Samaritan ER 24/7. Urgent care in Evans Mills (walk-in, near gate). OB/GYN delivers at Samaritan.
Food & Dining
Garland City Beer Works, Chatterbox Diner, Pete's Pizza, Sacketts Harbor Brewing. Growing food scene — not just chains.
Storage
ABC Self Storage (largest in NNY), Watertown Storage Solutions near gate, U-Haul, KO Storage. Climate-controlled options available.
Insider Picks
The spots you won't find on TripAdvisor

Garland City Beer Works

Best craft beer in Watertown. Period. Great vibe, food trucks rotate. Every military couple ends up here.

Sacketts Harbor Brewing

Lake Ontario views, outdoor seating, and solid beer. Perfect summer day trip with the family.

Dry Hill Ski Area

Tiny local ski hill, perfect for teaching kids. Cheap, no crowds, and 15 minutes from Watertown. Rent gear at MWR first.

Burnham Point State Park

Camping on the St. Lawrence River. Sunsets here will make you forget you're stationed in one of the coldest bases in the Army.

Your spouse deployed right after PCS?
It happens at Drum more than most bases. You're not alone — literally thousands of spouses have been exactly where you are. Your FRG, the Fort Drum Spouses Facebook group, and ACS are your lifeline. The neighbors will plow your driveway. The community will check on you. This is what makes Drum different.
