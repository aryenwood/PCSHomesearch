North Country Living

Discover the North Country

Fort Drum is more than a duty station. From the Thousand Islands to the Adirondacks — explore everything that makes this area worth calling home.

Family & Kids
Fort Drum is a great place to raise a family
Yes, even in winter. Here's where military families spend their time.

CYSS Youth Programs

On-post childcare, youth sports, summer camps, and after-school programs. Register through MilitaryChildCare.com as soon as you have orders — waitlists move slowly.

Flower Memorial Library

Watertown's public library has excellent children's programs, story hours, and a warm place to spend a snowy afternoon. Free with a library card.

Fort Drum MWR Family Programs

Indoor pools, bowling, movie nights, arts & crafts, and seasonal events. The MWR calendar is packed — check it monthly.

Playgrounds & Parks

Thompson Park in Watertown is the gem — zoo, pool, playground, and trails. On post, several housing areas have their own playgrounds.

Military Spouse Groups

Fort Drum has an active spouse community. FRG groups, spouse clubs, and Facebook groups are the fastest way to meet other families before you even arrive.

Indoor Winter Activities

Destiny USA mall (Syracuse, 90 min) has everything. Locally: bowling at Stripes, skating at Watertown Ice Arena, and the Sci-Tech Center in Watertown for kids.

Get Outside
The North Country is an outdoor playground
From lake days with the kids to snow days on the trails — there's something for every family.
Thousand Islands

1,864 islands spanning the US-Canada border. World-class fishing, kayaking, and the iconic Boldt Castle.

1 hr south
Adirondack Park

6 million acres of wilderness. Hiking, camping, and some of the best fall foliage in the Northeast.

1.5 hrs east
Tug Hill Plateau

The snowiest region east of the Rockies. Snowmobiling, cross-country skiing, and 300+ inches of snow per year.

30 min south
Lake Ontario

Charter fishing for salmon and trout. Henderson Harbor and Sackets Harbor are local favorites.

30 min west
Family Friendly
Plenty to keep the family busy

Sackets Harbor

Historic village on Lake Ontario. Battlefield tours, breweries, galleries, and the annual Can-Am Festival.

Fort Drum MWR

Family pools, youth sports, bowling, arts & crafts, and seasonal kid events — all on post. Don't sleep on the ski lodge at Whiteface for a family day trip.

Watertown Events

First Fridays downtown, Jefferson County Fair, Salmon Run Mall, plus a growing food scene.

Alex Bay & Clayton

Summer vacation towns on the St. Lawrence. Boat tours, ice cream shops, and the Antique Boat Museum.

Arriving alone with kids?
You're not the first — and you won't be the last. Fort Drum's spouse community is one of the strongest in the Army. Connect with your unit's Family Readiness Group before you arrive, and check the Fort Drum Spouses Facebook group. You'll have friends before you unpack your first box.
