## 1. MAJOR 2025-2026 PCS POLICY CHANGES

### HomeSafe Alliance Contract Canceled (June 2025)
- The $7.2 billion Global Household Goods Contract (GHC) with HomeSafe Alliance was **terminated for cause** -- widespread no-shows, delays, and damage complaints
- Military moves **reverted to the traditional system** through local Personal Property / Transportation Offices on each installation
- You now work directly with your **local Transportation Office (TO)** instead of a private contractor

### New Permanent Agency: Personal Property Activity (May 2026)
- Defense Secretary signed an order converting the PCS Joint Task Force into the **Personal Property Activity** -- a permanent organization at Scott AFB, IL
- Stands up officially **May 1, 2026** and reports directly to SecDef
- Goal: single chain of command, modern tech, real accountability for household goods moves

### 50% Cut to Discretionary PCS Moves (Starts FY2027)
- Pentagon ordered all services to cut discretionary PCS budgets by **50% by FY2030**
- **Timeline:** 10% cut in FY2027, 30% in FY2028, 40% in FY2029, 50% in FY2030
- "Discretionary" = career development, education-related, broadening assignments (NOT mission-required moves)
- **Summer 2026 PCS season operates under current rules** -- the 50% reduction does not begin until October 2026 (FY2027)
- The STAY Act (H.R. 6146, introduced Dec 2025) would codify this review -- not yet passed

### PPM Reimbursement Changes
- PPM (Personally Procured Move, formerly DITY) reimbursement is currently at **100% of government constructed cost**
- Briefly bumped to **130% during summer 2025** due to the HomeSafe contractor crisis
- PPM reimbursement is now based on rates the government **would have paid** under the traditional system
- **Warning:** Actual reimbursement may not cover your full out-of-pocket costs -- get a cost estimate from your Transportation Office BEFORE committing to a PPM

## 2. 2026 PCS ENTITLEMENTS -- KEY NUMBERS

### DLA (Dislocation Allowance)
- One-time payment to offset PCS costs (utilities hookup, deposits, etc.)
- **2026 rates increased 3.8%** over 2025
- Range: **$1,018.96** (E-1 without dependents) to **$6,385.58** (O-7+ with dependents)
- Partial DLA (for vacating government quarters): **$1,002.71**
- Paid automatically with PCS orders -- but verify with finance

### MALT (Mileage Allowance in Lieu of Transportation)
- **$0.205 per mile** in 2026 (for POV travel during PCS)
- Calculated using official distance between duty stations (not your actual odometer)

### Per Diem (En Route Travel)
- **Lodging:** $110/night
- **Meals & Incidentals:** $68/day
- **Service member:** 100% of rate
- **First dependent (spouse):** 75% of rate
- **Each additional dependent:** 50% of rate

### TLE (Temporary Lodging Expense) -- CONUS
- Up to **14 days** standard, split however you want between old/new duty station
- Up to **60 days** if relocating to an area with a **designated housing shortage**
- Maximum rate: **$290/day**
- Requires lodging receipts

- Up to an additional **$1,000** for business-related expenses
- Covers exam fees, registration fees, continuing education requirements

## 3. PCS TIMELINE CHECKLIST -- SPOUSE-FOCUSED

### As Soon As You Get Orders (8+ Weeks Out)
- [ ] Read orders carefully -- confirm report date, entitlements, and any special provisions
- [ ] **Apply for on-post housing immediately** if interested (Fort Drum waitlist: 0-35 days off-peak, 3-9 months during PCS season)
- [ ] Schedule HHG pickup through your **local Transportation Office** (not HomeSafe -- that's gone)
- [ ] Register on [militarychildcare.com](https://militarychildcare.com) for childcare at gaining installation -- waitlists can be months
- [ ] Request school records, medical records, dental records, immunization records, and pet records
- [ ] Contact the **Fort Drum School Liaison** at (315) 772-3214 for enrollment guidance
- [ ] Start decluttering -- every pound counts against your weight allowance
- [ ] Get a **PCS cost estimate** from your Transportation Office if considering a PPM
- [ ] Contact your **gaining installation's ACS Relocation Readiness** at (315) 772-6566

### 6 Weeks Out
- [ ] Schedule pre-move survey with Transportation Office (TMO will assess your HHG)
- [ ] Start using up pantry items, cleaning supplies, and open containers (movers won't pack them)
- [ ] If selling a home: list it NOW (6 weeks minimum lead time recommended)
- [ ] Give landlord **written SCRA notice** with copy of PCS orders (30 days before next rent due date)
- [ ] Notify schools of withdrawal date
- [ ] Schedule vet appointments for health certificates (required within 10 days of travel for most states)
- [ ] If your career requires a NY license: apply for **Temporary Practice Permit** through [NYSED](https://www.op.nysed.gov/about/military-spouses-relocated-new-york-state-expedited-licensing-services)

### 4 Weeks Out
- [ ] Confirm pack and pickup dates with Transportation Office
- [ ] Change address with USPS, banks, insurance, subscriptions
- [ ] Set aside a "do not pack" pile: important docs, medications, valuables, electronics, one week of clothes
- [ ] Photograph/video all belongings and serial numbers for claims purposes
- [ ] If doing a PPM: rent truck, get certified weight tickets (empty AND loaded)
- [ ] Transfer or cancel utilities, internet, and any local memberships
- [ ] Separate items movers won't ship: hazardous materials, cleaning supplies with bleach/ammonia, ammunition, propane tanks, open food, perishables, plants, fireworks

### Pack/Move Week
- [ ] Be present on pack day if at all possible -- label what goes where
- [ ] Take photos of everything BEFORE movers wrap it
- [ ] Get the **inventory sheet** and review it carefully before signing -- note any pre-existing damage
- [ ] Keep your "essentials" bag separate: documents, meds, phone chargers, snacks, change of clothes, kids' comfort items, pet supplies
- [ ] Clean the old place (or hire a move-out cleaning service)

### At Fort Drum (First 2 Weeks)
- [ ] In-process at your unit and complete installation newcomer orientation
- [ ] Visit ACS for welcome packet, lending closet items (FREE loaner pots, pans, dishes, chairs, car seats), and area orientation
- [ ] Enroll kids in school -- bring: birth certificate, immunization records, last report card, IEP if applicable, proof of residency
- [ ] Register for TRICARE at Guthrie Army Health Clinic: (315) 772-2778
- [ ] **File travel voucher within 30 days** of arrival at gaining installation
- [ ] Inspect HHG delivery carefully and note ALL damage on the delivery inventory
- [ ] File claims for damaged/missing items within **75 days** of delivery (or 75 days after HHG should have been delivered)

## 4. TIPS FOR HELPING KIDS THROUGH PCS

### Before the Move
- **Involve kids in every step** -- from choosing what to pack in their suitcase to researching the new area together online
- **Age-appropriate honesty:** Younger kids need reassurance and routine; teens need to feel heard and have their concerns validated
- **Create a goodbye ritual** -- let kids say proper farewells to friends, teachers, and familiar places
- Photograph their room, their school, their favorite spots so they have tangible memories

### School Transfer Tips
- Contact the **Fort Drum School Liaison** first: (315) 772-3214 -- they advocate for your kid
- NY honors the **Interstate Compact on Educational Opportunity for Military Children (MIC3)** -- your kid must be placed at the same grade level, and honors/AP/IB courses from the previous school must be honored
- **Pre-enroll remotely** -- NY law requires schools to allow advance enrollment for military children
- IEP/504 plans: the receiving school must provide **comparable services immediately** until they can re-evaluate
- Get a sealed copy of school records, last report card, and immunization records before you leave

### At the New Duty Station
- Sign up for the **Youth Sponsorship Program** -- matches your kid with a kid already at Fort Drum who shows them the ropes
- Register at Parent Central: (315) 772-8675 for youth sports, after-school programs, and activities
- **FRG (Family Readiness Group)** is the fastest way to meet other families -- ask your sponsor for the FRG leader contact
- Cornell Cooperative Extension's **4-H Military Youth** program offers STEM, leadership, and afterschool programs at 18 schools in Jefferson/Lewis counties

## 5. STRESS REDUCTION & MENTAL HEALTH RESOURCES

### Free Counseling (No Records, No Stigma)
- **Military OneSource:** 1-800-342-9647 (24/7) -- 12 free non-medical counseling sessions per issue. Covers stress, anxiety, relationship problems, parenting, grief, financial stress, and life transitions
- **MFLC (Military Family Life Counselor):** Free, confidential, short-term counseling. **No records kept. Does NOT affect security clearance.** Available at Fort Drum through ACS.
- **Military OneSource chat/video:** Available online at [militaryonesource.mil](https://www.militaryonesource.mil) for those who prefer not to call

### Relocation Readiness Program
- **Congressionally mandated** -- every installation must offer it
- Fort Drum ACS: (315) 772-6566 / 3241, located at 4330 Conway Road, Bldg P-4330
- Services: destination area information, housing cost analysis, childcare and school info, spouse employment help, cultural adaptation, community orientation, stress management counseling

### Spouse Employment & Education
- **MyCAA:** Up to $4,000 for certifications/degrees in portable careers (eligible: E1-E5, W1-W2, O1-O2 spouses)
- **MSEP:** 700+ corporate partners committed to hiring military spouses -- [msepjobs.militaryonesource.mil](https://msepjobs.militaryonesource.mil/msep/)
- **Employment Readiness Program:** Resume help, job search, license transfer assistance, job fairs -- available through Fort Drum ACS
- **Spouse relicensure:** Up to $1,000 reimbursement for license/cert costs + up to $1,000 for business expenses when PCS crosses state lines

### Family Advocacy Programs
- Parenting classes, relationship counseling, life skills training, domestic violence prevention
- Available through ACS at every installation

## 6. FORT DRUM-SPECIFIC TIPS

### Housing Reality
- **On-post (Mountain Community Homes):** 3,782 homes, 2-5 bedrooms. Rent = full BAH. Utilities included (huge winter savings). Apply at [fortdrummch.com](https://www.fortdrummch.com/apply-now/) or call (315) 955-6644. **Apply the moment you have orders** -- waitlists run 3-9 months during PCS season.
- **Off-post rentals:** Most families choose Watertown, Evans Mills, Calcium, or Dexter. A 3-bedroom typically runs **$1,300-$2,000/month**.
- **Budget for heating:** Off-post propane heat adds **$200-300/month** from October through April. On-post utilities are included -- that alone can save $1,500-3,000/year.
- **Natural gas vs. propane:** Watertown city has natural gas (National Grid). Evans Mills, Black River, Dexter, and rural areas are almost all propane -- which costs $1,000-2,000 MORE per year.
- **Verify before signing a lease:** Check [National Grid service availability](https://www1.nationalgridus.com/CheckServiceAvailability-StartService-NYL-RES) for the exact address.

### Winter Is No Joke
- **120+ inches of snow** annually. Lake-effect bands can dump 12-18 inches in hours.
- **Snow tires are not optional.** All-season tires are inadequate. Studded tires legal Oct 16 - Apr 30.
- **AWD/4WD strongly recommended.** RWD sedans are dangerous.
- **Remote start** is a quality-of-life must-have.
- **Budget $300-600/season** for driveway snow removal if off-post, or buy a quality snowblower.
- **Get furnace serviced in August/September** before heating season.
- **Propane pre-buy:** Lock in price between August 1 and October 1.

### Spouse Job Market
- Limited compared to bigger installations -- main options: healthcare (Samaritan Medical Center), retail/service, base civilian jobs, and **remote work**
- Fort Drum ACS Employment Readiness: [drum.armymwr.com](https://drum.armymwr.com/programs/employment-readiness-program/spouse-employment)
- Many spouses at Drum build remote careers or use MyCAA to certify in portable fields

## 8. THINGS MOVERS WON'T PACK (PLAN ACCORDINGLY)

- Hazardous materials (paint, paint thinner, gasoline, lighter fluid)
- Cleaning supplies containing bleach or ammonia
- Ammunition and firearms (varies -- confirm with TO)
- Propane tanks (even small camping ones)
- Perishable food and open containers
- Plants and live animals
- Fireworks
- Prescription medications (take with you)
- Important documents (take with you)
- Jewelry and high-value items (take with you)
